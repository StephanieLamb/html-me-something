2015 Trip to Europe

Overview of Trip

In August 2015, my boyfriend Nic and I went to Europe for a two and half week vacation. Our first stop was Madrid, Spain. We were in Madrid for a week and took a day trip to Valenica. From Madrid, we flew to Rome, Italy, where we also stayed for a week. While in Italy, we took a mini trip to Florence. Keep reading to hear about our adventures in each city!

Madrid, Spain

First things first, we were absolutely exhausted when we arrived in Madrid. We flew out from Chicago around 5pm and we arrived in Madrid around 6am their time. I barely got any sleep on the plane, so by the time we landed I had pretty much already been up for close to 24 hours. So trying to stay up until the evening hours was quite the challenge. After finally getting somewhat acclimated to the time difference we were able to go out and explore the city! Madrid has so much beautiful architecture and amazing parks. We spent most of our days just walking around looking at all the buildings, checking out museums and taking strolls through parks. Oh and eating of course! Spain is all about the food. Just a quick sidenote, Madrid is one of the cleaniest cities I've ever seen. We were blown away by how clean the sidewalks and streets were.

Valencia, Spain

Our day trip to Valencia was well worth it. Valencia has beautiful fine sand beaches and the birthplace of paella which of course we indugled in. Valencia also has a world famous aquarium that I would highly recommend to anyone.

Rome, Italy

Oh, Rome. All the pasta and pizza our hearts desired. We never had a bad meal. We ended up revisiting a couple of restaurants multiple times because their food was just too good. Sight seeing was just as awesome as the food. From the colosseum, to the Vatican, the Borghese, and countless more. Rome is definitely on our list of places to return!

Florence, Italy

Florence is a great city! I wish we were able to spend more than a day here. Little did we know Florence is known for its shopping. Just tiny stone streets filled with all different kinds of stores. Michelangelo's David is located in Florence, however we were unable to get tickets to go into the museum. We did make it to the Uffizi though which has several famous statues and paintings. One of Florence's most famous landmarks, the Duomo, was something to see. The church itself is gorgeous but the best part is climbing the two foot wide stair case all the way to the top for the view of all the city!

Overall, I would recommend any of these cities for anyone to visit! Each one is special and has so much to offer!
